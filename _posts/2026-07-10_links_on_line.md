Some links to content I've consumed recently that makes me think about the online world, life, the universe, and everything.
<!-- more -->

[Robin Sloan's self-destructing newsletter](https://www.robinsloan.com/winter-garden/magic-circle/): this particular edition has been sitting as an opened tab on my laptop since February. As always, the way this man thinks feels like the final evolution of the way I think. It's aspirational to read, always.

[The Alive Internet Theory](https://news.spencer.place/p/alive-internet-theory) from Spencer Chang on countering the dead internet (theory) by just... hanging out online. This scratches an itch in my brain about how I think of the Online (as a place, rather than a set of rules or a financial market).
I got this link from another Robin Sloan newsletter, I just can't figure out which one.

[Mia's Digital Diary](https://youtu.be/Xpp5WT_3uqQ?si=RrPqlgyPlkvcTyo9) on how she's designing tiny experiments throughout her year. The first experiment, "being my own algorithm" feels like the digital hygiene equivalent of not shopping while you're hungry. The video comes with a phenomenal ADHD-fueled preamble. This video, in turn, led me to:

- [Anne-Laure Le Cunff on Big Think](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3Bpv2LbM9-E) about the experimental mindset, and
- [This UN paper on the Attention Economy](https://www.un.org/sites/un2.un.org/files/attention_economy_feb.pdf) which I have yet to read.

[Hank Green on Effort](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AgBaU9mmceQ&list=TLPQMTAwNzIwMjZqoYi9NuvuRg&index=2), which I'm very upset that I found too late to order the t-shirt. He talks, in a very Hank Green way, about people being junk drawers and also the efforts we expend being Mola Mola eggs, which is his way of discussing what is better summarized by the summary of the video: "No effort is wasted." The chunk on how expending the effort to do a thing also helps in your Becoming (capitalizaion mine) made me think of...

[Brandon Sanderson on AI art](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mb3uK-_QkOo). Now, I am well aware that many of these pieces of content appear together in my feed because these are things that I am thinking about, and having smart people agree with me keeps me clicking. With that said, though, there's something very reassuring about seeing other people formulate the thoughts that are bouncing around in my head. I think that is how the best thoughts are thought... as a collective.
